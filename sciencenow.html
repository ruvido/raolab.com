P rotein function depends critically on the synergy between structure and dynamics. Protein dynamics often involves the interconversion of conformational states on a complex multi- dimensional free energy surface. It is very difficult to study such conformational transitions experimentally at an atomic level of detail, although techniques like single-molecule FRET, X-ray crystallography and NMR supply useful, but limited, information. Consequently, molecular dynamics simulations are playing an increasing role in determining the free energy surface, as a supplement to the experimental studies. Because of the large number of degrees of freedom involved, analysis of the results based on graph theory have been found to be useful. read more >>
The many abnormalities of water, such as the density maximum at 4 C, are related to the complex and highly dynamic network of hydrogen bonds it can form. There has been a long-standing debate about the relative importance of local ice-like structures, both in bulk water as well as in the context of the hydrophobic effect, and higher-density, disordered structures. Recent experiments have shown water to be structurally inhomogeneous. In order to characterize the structural inhomogeneity, we applied network analysis methods to molecular dynamics simulations of water. We found that the conformation- space-network of liquid water is highly structured, and that structural proper- ties of water molecules are spatially correlated on a 6 A length-scale. read more >>
